Impact of compilation by Integrated Development Environments in energy consumption during program execution

Brief Context

Experimental study that intends to investigate from an energetic point of view the efficiency of several relevant elements in Information Technologies. In particular, analyze the performance of executables generated by default from Software Development Tools. The results were obtained through software measurement of benchmarks using different compilation profiles. The resulting data is relative to 12 C programs, 18 development tools, 51 compilation profiles, 144 compilation flags, GCC compiler, Intel processor, among other elements. They are illustrated mainly from the perspective of their execution time, CPU and memory RAM energy consumption (individually and together) and the ratio between both factors (Energy/Time).

Testing Platform

The study was accomplished on a laptop Asus N56JN-DM127H, running under Linux:
  • Intel® Core i7-4710HQ up to 3.5 GHz, Haswell Family
  • 8 GB DDR3L 1600MHz
  • NVIDIA® GeForce® GT 840M, 2GB DDR3 VRAM
  • Ubuntu 16.04.4 LTS 64-bit (Linux Kernel 4.13.0-36)

Measurement Framework

Multifaceted tool that allows to carry out measurements of energy and time consumption with high accuracy through software. Uses the RAPL interface to read from Machine-Specific Registers information related with some hardware components during a period of time. In particular, it allows to obtain the CPU, RAM and GPU energy consumption for a given program during its compile and/or runtime. The measurement framework also provides a set of functionalities that help manage the measurement process and the complementary tasks.

Outputs

Information resulting from the measurement performed according to the methodology designed for the experimental study. Briefly, it consists of a set of more than 16000 files with data referring to 50 runs per program and compilation profile intended. Each file presents information about various elements for the execution in question, with particular emphasis on the CPU, RAM and GPU energy consumption values.

Processed Results

Processing result of the data obtained from the performed measurements according to the defined methodology. The information is presented from the point of view of several elements, namely through the perspective of the benchmarks, development tools and compilation profiles. The results illustrated are mainly focused on the execution time, CPU and memory RAM energy consumption (individually and together) and the ratio between both of these factors (energy consumption/time). In total, more than 1000 charts, tables and HTML pages are provided and also more than 100 rankings referring to information gathered.
